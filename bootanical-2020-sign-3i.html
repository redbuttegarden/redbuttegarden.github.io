Whipple's Cholla


Cylindropuntia whipplei 'Snow Leopard'

Cactus Family


This cultivar of the Utah native Cholla can grow as a low mat-forming or upright succulent shrub. It can be highly variable in appearance. It produces beautiful yellow flowers that contrast nicely with the thick, white, long spines.

Native Americans use this cactus ceremonially as a prayer stick. They also use the roots medicinally, as a salve for skin ailments. With the spines rubbed off, the fruit is eaten raw, or cooked in stew.
