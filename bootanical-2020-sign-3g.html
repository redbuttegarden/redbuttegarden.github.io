Yellow Horned Poppy


Glaucium flavum

Poppy Family


As its name suggests, Yellow Horned Poppy adorns bright yellow or orange flowers. The thick, leathery, wavy, bluish-grey leaves are coated in a layer of water retaining wax. The flowers give way to long, upright, thin, distinctive horn shaped capsules.


Similar to its family member, the Opium Poppy, Yellow Horned Poppy toxicity is high. All parts of this plant, including the seeds are poisonous. In high doses, it produces a sensation of initial euphoria much like being drunk. If dangerous doses are consumed, symptoms include brain damage and respiratory failure, resulting in death.
