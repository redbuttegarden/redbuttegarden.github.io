Adopt-A-Native-Elder Presents:
THE WEAVERS' STORIES


2017 CNN Hero and Founder of Adopt-A-Native-Elder, Linda Myers, will speak about her 30-year experience of serving traditional Elders living on the Navajo Reservation. Ms. Myers will share personal stories of the women who rely on weaving as a powerful tool in their fight against poverty. Elderly Navajo often struggle living on their own in very remote areas of the Reservation. Most of the traditional weavers are over 80 years old.

Event from 5:30-8PM


This will be a great opportunity to:

  • Listen to fascinating stories, as Native American Weavers talk about their lives and rugs
  • Gain knowledge of the spiritual significance of the different rug designs
  • Discover how traditional weavers use natural dye processes from plant sources like roots, berries, and bark
  • Learn about the current conditions facing many Navajo Elders

On-site demonstrations include:

  • Traditional Navajo Dancers perform from 5:30-6PM - right before the event
  • Loom Weaving Demonstrations
  • Explore the ethno-botanical ties between the Native American healing plants and many plants found in Red Butte Garden


Beautiful authentic traditional Navajo rugs and jewelry will be displayed and presented for sale.
100% of the proceeds go directly to the weavers. For many, this is the Weaver's sole means of support.
Free admission for this event only from 5:30-8PM.
