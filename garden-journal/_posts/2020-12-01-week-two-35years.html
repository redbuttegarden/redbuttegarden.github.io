This week learn more about the Red Butte Garden Outdoor Concert Series, Red Butte Garden Private Events, Oak Tunnel History, our School Programs, our Children's Programs, and the mighty Red Butte Garden Volunteers.


Red Butte Garden Outdoor Concert Series

From its beginning in the 1980s as a series of five or six alfresco summer concerts in the Garden on Sunday evenings, to the current 3,000-capacity amphitheatre, Red Butte Garden is the place to experience some of the best summer concerts in Utah.


Red Butte Garden Private Events

Garden spaces provide beautiful settings for weddings, receptions, parties, and photography. From casual to elegant, with breathtaking panoramic mountain and city views.

The same places are also peaceful, memorable, and lovely spaces to hold memorials, corporate events, and meetings.



The Oak Tunnel in the Natural Area

Over 100 years ago, Red Butte Canyon was the site of a sandstone quarry. Many early homes and buildings in Salt Lake City were built using this sandstone.

At that time the path of the Oak Tunnel was a railway to carry sandstone out of the quarry and to the mouth of the canyon. When the tracks were removed, the path remained raised, forcing the gambel oaks to grow up and around, forming a tunnel.


Red Butte Garden School Programs

Our School Programs team serves 25,000 students across the state each school year. Our goal is to deliver highly effective environmental and horticultural education programs that will have a meaningful and measurable impact on teachers and students of varying grade levels and backgrounds. All programs are offered at no cost to public and charter schools.


Red Butte Garden Children's Programs

Discover. Learn. Grow. at Red Butte Garden Summer Camp. Kids in kindergarten through sixth grade spend a week or two learning hands-on about plants, animals, art, science, and more.

Garden Advetures and Lil' Buds are short classes that a parent and a child can attend together to learn about the Garden and create a nature-inspired craft together.




Red Butte Garden Volunteers

The Volunteer program started out in 1988 with six people and today the Garden depends on almost 400 active horticulture, education, and event volunteers. We owe a great many thanks to them.
