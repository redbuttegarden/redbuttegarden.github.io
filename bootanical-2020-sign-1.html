BOO-tanically Wicked Plants

Sacred Datura


Datura wrightii

Nightshade Family

This native perennial has broad green deciduous leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually white and can be tinged purple. The big beautiful flowers bloom at night and close before noon the next day. They are the largest native flower in Utah. After pollination the flowers turn in to spiny green fruit capsules.

All parts of this plant are poisonous. It is named Sacred Datura for its special use in rituals by indigenous people all over the world. It is the most universally used hallucinogenic and medicinal plant known. It has been used both to poison enemies and to heal the sick. Shaman use Sacred Datura to transcend reality, entering other worlds, and going on magical flights. It enables the Shaman to come into contact with specific guardian spirits as well as the abilty to transform into other life forms such as a mountain lion or eagle.

Sacred Datura flowers are pollinated by the Hawk Moth (Manduca sexta) and the moth lays its eggs on the plant. The larvae consume the datura leaves once they are hatched. The Hawk Moths appear to become intoxicated after swimming in the datura pollen.
